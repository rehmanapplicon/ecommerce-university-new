EU as a brand: A Competitive Analysis

If a powerful brand strategy is where any Ecommerce business begins and ends, then the same has been incorporated in soul and spirit while structuring the Ecommerce University and some of which are as follows:

  • Ecommerce University franchisees companies have been structured to act as public facilitation companies instead of the traditional non-profit structures of the last 150 plus years. With more than 20,000 licenses to be issued to different cities around the world, it is a conglomerate of 20,000+ public facilitation companies with their own in-house stock exchange. Hence market capitalization would be built around a combination of tangible and non-tangible market indicators to establish a network of in-house traded, mutual benefit companies. The potential this business model offers in its brand value has thus been built around 20,000+ companies populating the internet with a powerful brand name and image.
  • EU developed a smart brand strategy to build a global impact. Essentially, the cost of operations has gone low not only for becoming part of a larger, parent publicly traded company of the future, but also the cost to support small and medium enterprise to collectively impact education and business alike.
  • “Ecommerce is a weapon of mass construction©”. It is essentially mankind’s first artificial intelligence backed attempt to catapult itself into quantum economics. Everything else has thus been principally strategized under this set of guidelines. We are attempting from micro to macro in a matter of minutes and boldly enhancing and supporting the demand and supply ratios.
  • The center core of financial planning is to lower expenditures in all areas of business to support low costs of education so that the consumer base can be enhanced. Non-affordability must become a thing of the past in real-time.
  • Companies have failed immensely in the past due to a clear lack of communication in their organizational readiness to adopt technology as a detrimental factor in their success. Hence, EU invented ORI (organization readiness intelligence), a process of removing all hindrances as a crucial element to its branding success.
